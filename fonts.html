Bold as Brass

As you can see Iâ€™ve made quite a bold type choice for the h1/display here. This is mainly because it seemed the greyscale colour scheme and lack of engaging visual content was begging for some character. This font is called KOSETSU-OUTLINE, and comes in a few variations with increasingly difficult levels of readability. To complement this display font, I chose to create visual contrast by using a well used, well loved and thoroughly tests ROBOTO typeface. This is incredibly readable and scales up and down well to suits various roles on the page. For the scale of type I chose dramatically wide intervals, doubling the size at every stage (octaves I guess?). This can require some layout planning but personally I love the strong effect it gives, especially when used with a bold display font, creating some beautiful contrast on the page.

Card Shark

The cards were design in a way to provide maximum usability with a second priority to minimalist aesthetic choices. The black background image was given to make provide uniformity to the cards visual structure between different image sizes, without having to distort or crop the images. The interaction buttons at the button were relocated to a div, which was then given a flex-direction of row in order to remove the implied hierarchy between the choices when they were stacked vertically. Furthermore, the colours were added to further increase the chance of the user making their correct choice quickly. The sans-serif font stands up well in the vastly different sizes here, and different opacities were used to denote differentiation and hierarchical order between the lines of text
